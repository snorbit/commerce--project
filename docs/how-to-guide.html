A Step-by-Step Guide to Finding the Best Deal

With so many different phone plans out there, it can be hard to know where to start. Here's a more detailed guide to help you make the right choice.

1. Understand Your Usage

Before you even start looking at plans, think about how you use your phone. Do you need a lot of data for streaming and social media? Do you make a lot of international calls? Most plans have unlimited standard national calls and texts, so data is often the biggest deciding factor.

2. Research Your Network Coverage

A great plan is useless if you can't get a signal. Check the coverage maps on the websites of the main network operators (Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone) to make sure you'll have service where you live, work, and hang out.

3. Decide on a Phone: BYO or Buy New?

  • Bring Your Own (BYO) Phone: If you're happy with your current phone, a pre-paid or SIM-only plan is a great way to save money.
  • Buy a New Phone Outright: This costs more upfront, but it gives you the freedom to switch plans whenever you want.
  • Buy on a Plan: This spreads the cost of the phone over a contract (usually 12, 24, or 36 months). It's a good way to get the latest phone without a huge upfront cost, but you're locked in for the contract period.

4. Compare Plans and Read the CIS

When you're comparing plans, look for the Critical Information Summary (CIS). This is a free document that all providers must offer, and it sets out the key details of the plan in a clear and simple way. Pay attention to:

  • Minimum Total Cost: The absolute minimum you'll have to pay over the life of the contract.
  • Data Allowance: And, importantly, what it costs if you go over your limit (though most plans now just slow your speed).
  • International Call and Roaming Costs: If you're planning to travel or call overseas, check these rates carefully.

5. Buying a Second-Hand Phone? Be Smart!

Buying a second-hand phone can be a great way to save money, but you need to be careful. Follow these tips from AMTA, the Australian Mobile Telecommunications Association:

  • Buy from a reputable seller: This is the best way to avoid scams.
  • Check the IMEI number: Before you buy, use AMTA's free IMEI checker to make sure the phone hasn't been reported as lost or stolen. A blocked phone won't work on Australian networks.
  • Get proof of ownership: Ask the seller for a receipt or other proof that they own the phone. This can protect you from a common scam where the seller reports the phone as stolen after they've sold it to you.
  • Check the phone in person: Test the screen, speakers, and cameras before you hand over any money.

6. Before You Sign the Contract

A phone contract is a legally binding agreement. Make sure you read it carefully and understand what you're signing up for. If you're not sure about something, ask questions or get advice from a parent or trusted friend. Missing payments on your phone contract can affect your credit rating, which can make it harder to get loans in the future.
