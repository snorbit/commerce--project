Do You Really Need Those Extras?

When you buy a new phone, you'll often be offered a range of add-ons, from insurance to screen protectors. But are they worth the extra money? Here's what you need to know.

Phone Insurance: Is It Worth It?

In most cases, the answer is no. While phone insurance promises to repair or replace your phone if it's lost, stolen, or damaged, it often comes with a high monthly fee and a hefty deductible (the amount you have to pay when you make a claim). In many cases, you'll also receive a refurbished phone as a replacement, not a brand new one.

Instead of paying for expensive insurance, consider these alternatives:

  • Get a good case and screen protector: This is the simplest and most effective way to protect your phone from drops and scratches. A sturdy case from a brand like Otterbox might seem expensive, but it's a one-off cost that's much cheaper than monthly insurance payments.
  • Check your credit card: Some credit cards offer free phone insurance if you use them to pay your monthly phone bill.
  • Use a local repair shop: If you do crack your screen, a local repair shop will often be able to fix it for a fraction of the cost of an insurance deductible.
  • Create an emergency fund: Instead of paying an insurance company each month, put that money into your own savings account. If you do need to repair or replace your phone, you'll have the money saved up. And if you don't, the money is still yours!

Screen Protectors: A Smart Investment

A screen protector is a thin layer of plastic or glass that you apply to your phone's screen. It's designed to absorb the impact of a drop and prevent scratches. While it won't make your phone indestructible, it's a cheap and effective way to protect the most fragile part of your device. For the small cost, a screen protector is almost always a worthwhile investment.
