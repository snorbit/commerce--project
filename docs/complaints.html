Have a Problem? Here's How to Solve It.

Sometimes, things don't go as planned. You might have a problem with your new phone, your bill, or your service. If that happens, it's important to know your rights and how to make a complaint.

Step 1: Contact Your Provider First

The first and most important step is to contact your mobile provider directly. Give them a chance to fix the problem. You can usually find a dedicated complaints section on their website with a phone number or email address. Explain the issue clearly and tell them what you'd like them to do to resolve it.

Step 2: Escalate to the Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman (TIO)

If you're not happy with your provider's response, or if they haven't fixed the problem, you can contact the TIO. The TIO is a free and independent dispute resolution service for phone and internet complaints.

How the TIO Process Works:

  1. You make a complaint: You can do this online or over the phone. Tell them about your problem and what a fair solution would look like.
  2. Referral to your provider: The TIO will refer your complaint to a dedicated team at your provider and give them 10 business days to work with you to solve it.
  3. Investigation: If the problem is still not resolved, the TIO can investigate further. They act like an umpire, not taking sides, to find a fair and reasonable outcome.
  4. Binding Decision: If necessary, the TIO can make a decision that is binding on your provider.

You can contact the TIO through their website or by phone:

  • Website: www.tio.com.au
  • Phone: 1800 062 058

What About the ACCC?

The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) doesn't resolve individual complaints. However, if you believe a provider is systematically doing the wrong thing (like widespread misleading advertising), you can report it to the ACCC. This helps them enforce the rules and protect all consumers.
