Your Questions Answered

What's the difference between a pre-paid and a post-paid plan?

A pre-paid plan is one where you pay for your service upfront. A post-paid plan (also known as a contract) is one where you pay for your service at the end of each month. You can learn more on our Contracts vs. Pre-Paid page.

Can I keep my phone number if I switch providers?

Yes! In Australia, you have the right to keep your phone number when you switch providers. This is called 'porting'. When you sign up with a new provider, they will ask you if you want to port your existing number.

What does 'data banking' mean?

Data banking, or data rollover, allows you to save your unused data from one month and use it in the next. This is a great feature if your data usage varies from month to month.

What happens if I go over my data limit?

On most modern plans, you won't be charged extra if you go over your data limit. Instead, your data speed will be 'throttled' (slowed down) to a much lower speed until your next billing cycle. This is usually fast enough for sending messages and emails, but not for streaming video.

Do I need to get my phone repaired by the manufacturer?

No, you don't. There are many independent repair shops that can fix common problems like cracked screens, often for a much lower price than the manufacturer. However, be aware that using an unauthorised repairer may void your warranty.
