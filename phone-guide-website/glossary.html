Key Terms to Know

Data
The amount of internet you can use on your phone when you're not connected to Wi-Fi. It's usually measured in gigabytes (GB).
Data Banking
A feature that lets you save your unused data from one month to use in the future. Also known as data rollover.
Deductible
The amount of money you have to pay out-of-pocket when you make an insurance claim before the insurance company will pay.
eSIM
A digital SIM that allows you to activate a cellular plan from your carrier without having to use a physical SIM card.
Porting
The process of transferring your existing mobile number from one provider to another.
Post-paid
A type of mobile plan where you receive a bill at the end of each month for the service you've used. Also known as a contract plan.
Pre-paid
A type of mobile plan where you pay for your service in advance.
SIM Card
A small chip inside your phone that connects it to a mobile network.
Throttling
The practice of slowing down your internet speed after you've used up your monthly data allowance.
TIO (Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman)
A free and independent service that resolves complaints about phone and internet services in Australia.
